INTRODUCTION

Khokona (Translation :'Telling while Weeping’.)is a small yet extremely authentic traditional Newari Village located right beside the heart of the nation in Lalitpur (Patan). It is known as the 'land of oil' commonly. The village is the rich in mustard oil farms.

Further, the agricultural village is famous for its pure and locally produced mustard oil all over Nepal. It is also the 1st place to have been lit up with electricity in Nepal.

Shree Rudrayani Mandir

The Rudrayani temple is a three storeyed tiered temple lin Khokana. It is the temple of Goddess Rudrayani worshipped as the manifestation of Goddess Durga and the mother goddess of the town. The temple's most distinctive architectural feature is its highly projected inclined lattice work in the second storey. The temple was constructed during the reign of Amar Malla during 1570 B.S.
